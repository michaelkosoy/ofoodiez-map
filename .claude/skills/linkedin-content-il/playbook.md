# LinkedIn distribution & formatting playbook — Ofir's proven rules

Ofir's own battle-tested rules for making a post perform (from his post *"10 tips for founders building
a personal brand on LinkedIn in 2026"*). The skill **applies** the rules that shape the written post and
**surfaces** the rest as a publishing checklist with the deliverables. Don't drop any of them.

**The worldview.** In 2026 a personal brand is not ego — it's the **strongest distribution channel** a
founder has. ~1% of active LinkedIn users post weekly, and they capture billions of impressions. As a
builder it's easy to disappear into the product and neglect distribution. Don't. When you consistently
give value in public, the people you need — hires, partners, first customers — come to you on their own.

## The 10 rules

1. **Give real value, never sell.** The audience is tired of "we raised" and "I spoke at a conference"
   posts. Write about *the audience's* problems. Be the one who gives. → *core ethos (SKILL.md §1/§3).*

2. **Ground in research, don't guess.** Ofir scans Reddit by topic to write about real pain — exactly
   what this skill's Stage 5 (§10) does. Keep posts anchored in what actually hurts people. → *§10.*

3. **Write for Saves.** Saves are the strongest algorithm signal today. Build posts worth saving (a
   rule, a framework, two real numbers). Optionally end with a **tasteful** save nudge ("worth saving
   if you're rolling this out") — never baity, never "thoughts?". → *applied in §16 scoring.*

4. **Optimize for engagement, not impressions.** Impressions are vanity; the **right people engaging**
   tells you the message landed with the audience that matters. → *what "good" means (§16).*

5. **The 15-second rule (hook + dwell).** A post skimmed under ~3s gets buried; one that holds
   attention past ~15s gets boosted. Open with a **curiosity/claim line that earns the "…read more"
   click**, break with whitespace, and make the body reward the dwell. The first line is the whole
   ballgame. → *applied in §15 (write) and §16 (the hook bank).*

6. **The first 15 minutes decide everything.** Post when the audience is awake and browsing, and reply
   to **every** comment in the first hour to build momentum. → *publishing checklist deliverable.*

7. **PDF carousels (~3–5x).** Carousels currently pull roughly 3–5x the impressions and saves of text.
   When the topic is a multi-step framework, comparison, or checklist, **lean toward a carousel**:
   8–12 slides, with the **value on slide 1** (not a title slide). Ofir under-uses these and they work —
   bias toward recommending one. → *strengthens §17 carousel deliverable.*

8. **Comments are the early growth engine.** Spend ~20 min/day leaving substantive comments on strong
   voices in the field — early on this drives more profile visits than your own posts. → *checklist.*

9. **No links in the body.** An external link in the post still suppresses reach. Keep the post
   **link-free**; put sources/links in the **first comment**, posted right after, and lean on it only
   once the post starts moving. → *this is why proof/source links go in the "First comment" deliverable
   (§17 #9), never in the body.*

10. **Coin your own phrase.** Develop signature terminology/idioms that become *yours*. → *voice.md
    signature motifs.*

## Also part of the voice

Reply to every comment — it respects the people who showed up and signals a real human; Ofir's comment
threads are often as valuable as the post, and he learns from them. Treat replies as part of the
content. (See `voice.md`.)
